Highlighted Mars Rovers

Curiosity

Launched in November 2011 and landing in August 2012, Curiosity is a car-sized rover that has been exploring Gale Crater on Mars. Its mission focuses on determining if Mars ever had conditions suitable for microbial life. Curiosity carries a powerful suite of instruments, including cameras, spectrometers, and a laser that vaporizes rocks to analyze their composition.

Spirit

Spirit launched on June 10, 2003, and landed on Mars on January 4, 2004. It was part of NASA's Mars Exploration Rover mission and was designed for a 90-day mission but lasted over six years. Spirit made key discoveries, including evidence of ancient volcanic activity and signs that water once existed in the region it explored.

Opportunity

Opportunity was part of the Mars Exploration Rover mission and launched in July 2003, landing on Mars in January 2004. Originally designed for a 90-day mission, it exceeded expectations and operated for nearly 15 years. During its time on Mars, Opportunity made important discoveries about ancient water activity and mineral composition.

Background

The NASA Mars rover program is a pioneering effort to explore the Red Planet using robotic vehicles designed to traverse its surface and conduct scientific experiments. Beginning with Sojourner in 1997, the program has since launched a series of increasingly advanced rovers, including Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance.
